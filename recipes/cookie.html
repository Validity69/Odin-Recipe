THIS IS MY COOKIE RECIPE

Description

Cookies are sweet, baked treats made from a dough of flour, sugar, butter, and other ingredients like chocolate chips, nuts, or dried fruits. They come in various textures, from soft and chewy to crisp and crunchy, depending on the recipe and baking method. Originating from Persia, cookies have evolved into countless variations worldwide, including classics like chocolate chip, oatmeal raisin, and shortbread. Their versatility makes them a beloved snack for all ages, perfect for pairing with milk, coffee, or tea. Beyond their taste, cookies hold cultural and emotional significance, often tied to traditions, celebrations, and homemade comfort. They are popular during holidays like Christmas, where families bake and decorate them together, creating cherished memories. With the rise of artisanal and gourmet baking, cookies continue to evolve, incorporating unique flavors and ingredients such as matcha, sea salt caramel, and even plant-based alternatives. Whether store-bought or homemade, cookies remain an enduring symbol of simple joy and indulgence.

    Wet ingredients

  • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs

    • Dry ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups (315g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups (340g) chocolate chips
    • 1 cup (200g) brown sugar
  • ½ cup (100g) white sugar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt

    DIRECTIONS:

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream the butter and sugars – In a large bowl, beat the butter, brown sugar, and white sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add eggs and vanilla – Mix in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Combine dry ingredients – In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt.
  5. Mix everything – Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until combined. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  6. Scoop and bake – Drop spoonfuls of dough onto the baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are golden brown.
  7. Cool and enjoy – Let cookies rest on the sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack.
